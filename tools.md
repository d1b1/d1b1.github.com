<h2 class="mt-5 mb-4">
  Decision Framework: When to Use Traditional Tools
</h2>

<p class="fs-5">
  As a CEO, your tech stack decisions impact cost, speed, risk, and team requirements. Here's a simple framework:
</p>

<div class="row mb-4">
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <h4>Choose Traditional Tools When:</h4>
    <ul class="fs-5">
      <li><b>Performance is critical</b>—You need maximum speed or efficiency</li>
      <li><b>Custom requirements</b>—Low-code platforms can't meet your specific needs</li>
      <li><b>You have the team</b>—Skilled developers available and budgeted</li>
      <li><b>Long-term investment</b>—This is core IP that justifies the build cost</li>
      <li><b>Integration complexity</b>—You need deep, custom integrations</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="col-md-6">
    <h4>Consider Low-Code/AI Tools When:</h4>
    <ul class="fs-5">
      <li><b>Speed to market</b>—You need to ship faster</li>
      <li><b>Limited budget</b>—You can't afford a full dev team</li>
      <li><b>Standard workflows</b>—Your needs fit common patterns</li>
      <li><b>Rapid iteration</b>—You need to test and change quickly</li>
      <li><b>Reduced risk</b>—You want less technical debt and maintenance</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<p class="fs-5 mb-4">
  The best approach often combines both: use low-code/AI for speed and cost efficiency, and traditional tools 
  where you need maximum control or performance. I help you make these decisions based on your business 
  priorities, not just technical preferences.
</p>
